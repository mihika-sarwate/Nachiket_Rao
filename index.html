Myths of Hypnotherapy

“Hypnotherapy means losing control or becoming unconscious.”

Facts of Hypnotherapy

Hypnotherapy is a state of deep relaxation with heightened awareness. You remain fully conscious, aware, and in control throughout the session.

Myths of Hypnotherapy

“A hypnotherapist can make you say or do things against your will.”

Facts of Hypnotherapy

Hypnotherapy cannot override your values or free will. The subconscious accepts only what feels safe, appropriate, and aligned with you.

Myths of Hypnotherapy

“Only weak-minded or highly suggestible people can be hypnotized.”

Facts of Hypnotherapy

Hypnotherapy works best with individuals who are self aware, reflective, and willing to participate consciously in the process.

Myths of Hypnotherapy

“Hypnotherapy is magical, mystical, or supernatural.”

Facts of Hypnotherapy

Hypnotherapy is a structured therapeutic process that uses focused attention and deep relaxation. Similar natural trance states occur during meditation, deep focus, or moments of complete absorption.

Myths of Hypnotherapy

“Hypnotherapy produces instant results for everyone.”

Facts of Hypnotherapy

Some people experience noticeable shifts quickly, while others require more time. Healing depends on emotional depth, readiness, and how safely the subconscious allows change to unfold.

Myths of Hypnotherapy

“You will not remember anything from a hypnotherapy session.”

Facts of Hypnotherapy

Memories are not erased. Instead, the emotional charge connected to past experiences is gently released so they no longer disturb the present.

Myths of Hypnotherapy

“I will not be able to remember anything that I saw in Hypnosis.”

Facts of Hypnotherapy

Most people remain aware and remember the session clearly. You can hear, respond, and reflect throughout the process.
